My Photography Journey

I got into photography back in 2019 when I took a casual DSLR course in India. What started as just learning how a camera works quickly turned into something I genuinely love. I started shooting everything around me — my sister, sunsets, nature, my dog, and even my mom's fashion work. My dog is probably my most photographed subject; he's my favorite thing to capture. There was something about freezing a moment exactly as I saw it that kept pulling me back, Especially during traveling and exploring a new city in the summer

What I find most interesting about photography is that I feel like I see the world through a photographic lens, even without a camera in hand. I can usually tell when a shot is going to be good — the lighting, the composition, the timing. Of course, what my eyes perceive is always a little different from what the camera captures, since the way we see the world is so much richer than any lens can replicate. But that's what makes it exciting — I'm always trying to bridge that gap and capture the moment as close to how I experienced it as possible.

These days I shoot more casually using my digital camera and my iPhone. I don't always have my DSLR with me like I did back home in India, but I still find myself constantly framing shots in my head. Whether it's a sunset over the Boston skyline or a candid moment with friends, I try to capture it whenever I can.

Things I Love to Photograph
